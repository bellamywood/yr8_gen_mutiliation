🔵 Male Circumcision
⏱ 5 mins
Definition Male circumcision is the surgical removal of some or all of the foreskin which covers the end of the penis. It may be done for medical reasons or for religious and cultural reasons, particularly in boys of the Jewish and Muslim faiths. It may be performed shortly after birth, during childhood, or around puberty as a rite of passage.
  • Around 30% of males worldwide are circumcised (WHO data)
  • Makes no difference to a man's ability to urinate, ejaculate or enjoy sex
  • It is NOT against the Law in the UK
Female Circumcision (FGM) FGM is the surgical removal of some or total removal of the external female genitalia, or other injury to the female genital organs. This is AGAINST the Law in the UK — the offender can face 14 years in prison, or a fine, or both.

🌿 External Body Parts — What's Natural?
⏱ 3 mins
💡 Let's bust some myths!
Everyone's sexual body parts are as varied and unique as other body parts, like noses. There is no single "normal" appearance — the correct word is natural.

Just as faces and noses vary enormously between people, so do all body parts. Variation is completely natural and healthy.

🧠 Reflect: Why do you think people might feel anxious about being "different"? How might media images contribute to this?

Teacher note: Please use the term "natural" rather than "normal" throughout this lesson.

⚠️ FGM — Facts & Risks
⏱ 2 mins
What is FGM? Female genital mutilation (FGM) is a criminal act carried out in some communities, typically on girls under the age of 15. The female genitals are cut, injured, or changed with no medical reason. There are no benefits to FGM — only serious risks.

✅ FGM & The Law — Answers
🚫 ALL of the actions listed — except "Performing FGM on yourself" — are illegal for UK citizens and permanent UK residents.

FGM is a form of serious violence. If the victim is under 16, it is also a form of child abuse.

🛂 The FGM Health Passport A "Statement Opposing FGM" is available from the UK government website. It outlines what FGM is, the legislation and penalties, and available support. You can show this to family members when travelling abroad. It makes clear that FGM carries a maximum penalty of 14 years in prison.

The statement is available in: Amharic, Arabic, Farsi, French, Malay, Somali, Swahili, Tigrinya, Turkish, Urdu, Welsh.

✅ Male Circumcision

  • Can be carried out for medical reasons (e.g. relieving pain when urinating)
  • Can be carried out for religious reasons (e.g. Judaism)
  • Carried out to reduce health risks
  • Does not impact male reproductivity
  • Not illegal in the UK

🚫 Female Circumcision (FGM)

  • Carried out as a form of male domination — to prevent women enjoying sex
  • Has nothing to do with religion
  • Can leave girls and women with life-long pain
  • Can be life-threatening and cause complications in childbirth
  • Illegal in the UK — up to 14 years in prison

📖 Scenario — Waris' Story
⏱ 10 mins — Reading & Questions
The time had come for my oldest sister Aman, to be circumcised. Like all younger siblings, I was envious, jealous that she was entering this grown-up world that was still closed to me. Aman was a teenager, much older than the normal age for circumcision, but so far, the timing had never been right. As my family travelled Africa in an endless cycle, we had somehow missed the gypsy woman who performed this ancient ritual. My father was growing concerned, because Aman was reaching marriageable age, but no marriage could take place unless she had been properly "fixed"…

All young girls in my country anxiously await the ceremony that will make their transformation from being a little girl to becoming a woman. When I heard that the old gypsy was coming to circumcise Aman, I wanted to be circumcised too. The day before the event, I begged my mother, tugging at her arm, "Mama, do us both together. Come on Mama, do both of us tomorrow."
